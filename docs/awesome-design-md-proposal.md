## Additional Details

Arcade Skill is a maker-owned retro developer waiting-room arcade. It turns the
short wait while Codex, Claude Code, tests, installs, or builds are running into
a tiny browser arcade break.

Arcade Skill turns the short wait while Codex, Claude Code, tests, installs, or
builds are running into a tiny browser arcade break. The visual system combines
a terminal-status surface with a compact retro cabinet, real game-state
signals, 4-8px radii, and a warm paper/dark-screen palette. It is intentionally
not a generic games portal or a SaaS hero with game copy.

The reusable pattern is the **agent waiting room**: a product can acknowledge
idle time without pulling the user into a feed or a full gaming session. The
screen shows the active command, a recognisable pixel player, floor/health/seed
state, and a direct route back to play. Payment and ranking copy are kept
separate from the primary play action: Stripe support never buys health,
revives, or leaderboard advantage.
